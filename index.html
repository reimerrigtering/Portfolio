Be Social, Make Friends

Be Social, Make Friends

I designed a social gathering space for teenagers to meet on Sunday evenings so they can meet up and play games. This is a space created to give teenagers a meetin place and is open to everybody, the idea is to stimulate contact between these teenagers and take them outside their own daily bubble. This is all to combat lonelyness, give new perspectives and give teenagers a place of community with people of their own age. They help and support each other.

This space started as a messy upstairs area of a church in the city center of Heemskerk. Over a period of about 3 months I designed and build, with the help of some friends, the entire space. Taking into acount budget, different space uses and the fact that the church is a monument. This project took sketches, pitching to the church board for budget, 3D modeling and eventualy building everything and so many more things.

Curious 'bout Art

Curious 'bout Art

This artwork was made in a team with the goal to stimulate senses. Using sound, lights and haptics to pull the user in and let the surroundings fade away. Using felt to stimulate touch within the artwork, a headphone connected to a programmed synthesizer created sounds based on the position of your hands within the artwork as did the lights.

The artwork has "invisible strings" to play, 3 vertical invisible lines where your hands create and mix sounds and lights. Different heights created different tones within octaves and different string corresponded to different colors. When playing multiple strings simultaniously the sound and lights would combine to totaly new sounds and colors.

Show Don't Tell

Show Don't Tell

Over time, I equipped myself with multiple different tools to show ideas instead of telling them. Using basic skeching on paper, skethcing digitally or 3D modeling, all of them create a "show, don't tell"-environment for a team of people to work in.

Conveying information within your team is extremely important, and in my experience, trying to explain an idea with only words is a mayor cause for miscommunication. Showing an idea with models, sketches of small builds is the absolute best way to explain an idea to team members, and has the added benefit of (in most cases) immediatly exposing weaknesses in a design or concept.

If an idea is shown, investors, interested parties and team members can ask better questions, creating the best oppertunities for a design to improve.

Quantum-English

Quantum-English

The Quantum Poiesis project is a project where I did the coding for an artist for this piece. It is the first step towards a quantum computer speaking old English.

This piece runs a quantum computer simulation based on a quantum computer of QuTech. The data from these quantum bits are plotted on a sphere, which in turn get translated into letters and sounds. Which in turn get spoken alout by the computer using a Text-to-Speech engine. To make this work continously on an exibition this system needed to be robust and should be able to run indefinetly without human intervention.

This pieces uses a combination of Python for all the background tasks and Kotlin using OPENRNDR for the forground.
