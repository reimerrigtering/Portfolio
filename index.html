Be Social, Make Friends

Be Social, Make Friends

I designed a social gathering space for teenagers to meet on Sunday evenings so they can meet up and play games. This is a space created to give teenagers a meeting place and is open to everybody. The idea is to stimulate contact between these teenagers and take them outside their own daily bubble. This is all to combat lonelyness, give new perspectives and give teenagers a place of community with people of their own age. This way thay have oppertunities to help and support each other.

This space started as a messy upstairs area of a church in the city center of Heemskerk. Over a period of about 3 months I designed and built, with the help of some friends, the entire space. Taking into acount budget, different space uses and the fact that the church is a monument which brings its own restrictions. This project took sketches, pitching to the church board for budget, 3D modeling and eventualy building everything and so many more things to be brought to an end.

Curious 'bout Art

Curious 'bout Art

This artwork was made in a team with the goal to stimulate senses. Using sound, lights and haptics to pull the user in and let the surroundings fade away. Using felt to stimulate touch within the artwork, a headphone connected to a programmed synthesizer created sounds based on the position of your hands within the artwork as did the lights.

The artwork has "invisible strings" to play. 3 vertical invisible lines where your hands create and mix sounds and lights. Different heights created different tones within octaves and different string corresponded to different colors. When playing multiple strings simultaniously the sound and lights would combine to totaly new sounds and colors.

VR Designing

VR Designing

With a group of 3 people I designed a Mars rover using VR sketching. This rover was designed by moving in VR and sketching the rover in the surrounding room. After a lot of different iterations and changes this was the end product.

By first creating a frame of the entire rover scale the basic shape was established. Then by using a long process the entire frame was closed using sheets to connect all ribs of the frame, while making sure objects had a double wall so to not be infinitely thin and create an inside living space. We added all necessary objects to create a multi-day use rover to be used on missions in the future.

Lastly the rover was rendered within Blender to create the best visuals and present the concept. Using different angles and views the rover was displayed within a digital environment.

Quantum-English

Quantum-English

The Quantum Poiesis project is a project where I did the coding for an artist for this piece. It is the first step towards a quantum computer speaking old English.

This piece runs a quantum computer simulation based on a quantum computer of QuTech. The data from these quantum bits are plotted on a sphere, which in turn get translated into letters and sounds. They are then spoken alout by the computer using a Text-to-Speech engine. To make this work continously on an exibition this system needed to be robust and should be able to run indefinetly without human intervention.

This pieces uses a combination of Python for all the background tasks and Kotlin using OPENRNDR for the forground.
